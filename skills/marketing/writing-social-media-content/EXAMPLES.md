## Example 5: Twitter/X thread

**Input:**
- Platform: Twitter/X
- Purpose: Educational content
- Topic: 5 pricing page mistakes

**Output:**
```
1/ Your pricing page is probably losing you customers.

5 mistakes I see constantly (and how to fix them):

2/ Mistake 1: Too many options.

Three tiers max. More than that creates decision paralysis.

If you have 5+ plans, you're not segmentingâ€”you're confusing.

3/ Mistake 2: Features nobody understands.

"10GB of storage" means nothing to most buyers.

Translate features into outcomes: "Room for 50,000 documents."

4/ Mistake 3: Hiding the price.

"Contact sales" for pricing signals "expensive" and "complicated."

If you can show price, show price. Remove friction.

5/ Mistake 4: No social proof near the buy button.

Logos and testimonials should be visible when someone's deciding.

Trust reduces at the moment of commitment.

6/ Mistake 5: Burying the recommended plan.

Make your best-fit option obvious. "Most popular" badges work.

Don't make customers do math to figure out what they need.

7/ TL;DR:
- Max 3 tiers
- Outcomes, not features
- Show price
- Add social proof
- Highlight the right choice

What pricing page patterns have worked for you?
```

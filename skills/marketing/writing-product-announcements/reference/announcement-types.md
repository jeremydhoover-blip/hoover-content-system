# Announcement types

## Classification matrix

| Type | Urgency | User action required | Tone |
|------|---------|---------------------|------|
| Launch | Low | Optional (try it) | Celebratory, benefit-focused |
| Update | Low | None | Informative, brief |
| Deprecation | Medium | Required by deadline | Clear, helpful, not apologetic |
| Breaking change | High | Required immediately | Direct, specific, urgent |
| Incident resolution | Medium | None (awareness) | Factual, reassuring |
| Beta/Early access | Low | Optional (opt in) | Inviting, exclusive |

## Launch announcements
**Purpose**: Introduce new features, products, or capabilities.

**Required elements**:
- Clear name of what's launching
- What users can now do (capabilities, not technical specs)
- Availability scope (all users, specific plans, regions)
- How to access or get started

**Tone**: Enthusiastic but substantive. Lead with benefit.

## Update announcements
**Purpose**: Communicate improvements to existing features.

**Required elements**:
- What improved and how
- User benefit of the change
- Whether action is needed (usually no)

**Tone**: Brief, matter-of-fact. Don't oversell small changes.

## Deprecation announcements
**Purpose**: Notify users that a feature, API, or behavior is being removed.

**Required elements**:
- What is being deprecated
- Sunset date (specific, not "soon")
- Reason for deprecation (brief)
- Recommended alternative
- Migration steps or resources
- Support contact for questions

**Tone**: Direct, helpful. Acknowledge impact without excessive apology.

**Lead time guidelines**:
- Minor feature: 30 days minimum
- Major feature: 90 days minimum
- API version: 6 months minimum
- Breaking infrastructure change: 6-12 months

## Breaking change announcements

- When the change takes effect
- Who is affected (specific criteria)
- Required action with steps
- Deadline for action
- Help resources

**Tone**: Urgent, specific. No ambiguity about impact or timeline.

## Incident resolution announcements
**Purpose**: Close the loop on outages, security incidents, or major bugs.

**Required elements**:
- What happened (factual summary)
- Impact scope and duration
- Root cause (if known and shareable)
- What we fixed
- Preventive measures (if applicable)

**Tone**: Factual, accountable. Don't over-apologize but acknowledge impact.

## Beta and early access
**Purpose**: Invite users to try pre-release features.

**Required elements**:
- What the feature does
- Current limitations or known issues
- How to enable or request access
- How to provide feedback
- Expected timeline to general availability (if known)

**Tone**: Inviting, transparent about status. Set appropriate expectations.

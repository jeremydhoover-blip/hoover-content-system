# Templates

## Default output: Thought leadership article

```md
# [Provocative, perspective-driven headline]

[Opening hook: counterintuitive observation, striking data point, or vivid scenario — 2-3 sentences max]

[Thesis statement: the core argument in one clear sentence]

## The conventional view

[Describe what most people believe or how the industry currently operates — establish the baseline the reader recognizes]

## What's actually happening

[Present evidence that challenges the conventional view — data, examples, or observed patterns]

## Why this matters

[Explain the implications — what's at stake if the reader doesn't update their thinking]

## The path forward

[Actionable insight or strategic reframe — what should leaders do differently]

---

*[Author bio: 1-2 sentences establishing relevant credentials]*
```

## Alternative: Contrarian take format

For pieces that directly challenge popular opinion:

```md
# [Statement that challenges consensus]

Everyone says [conventional wisdom]. Here's why that's wrong.

## The standard argument

[Steelman the opposing view — show you understand it]

## The flaw

[Identify the specific logical gap, missing context, or changed circumstance]

## The real picture

[Present your alternative framework with evidence]

## What this means for you

[Practical implication for the reader's decisions]
```

## Alternative: Future-forward format

For trend prediction and strategic foresight:

```md
# [Future state headline — specific and time-bound if possible]

## Where we are

[Current state — brief, factual]

## The forces at play

[2-3 trends or shifts driving change]

## Where this leads

[Projected outcome with reasoning]

## Preparing now

[Actions to take before the shift]
```

## Headline patterns

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Counterintuitive claim | "Why Your Best Engineers Should Stop Coding" |
| Future declaration | "The End of the Dashboard Era" |
| Hidden truth | "What No One Tells You About Scaling Teams" |
| Reframe | "Technical Debt Isn't Debt — It's Deferred Learning" |
| Permission-giving | "It's OK to Ship Imperfect Products" |

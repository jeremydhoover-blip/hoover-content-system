# Templates

## Default output: Comprehensive guide structure

```md
# [Title]

[Executive summary: 2-3 sentences stating what this covers and who it's for]

## Contents
- [Section 1 title](#section-1)
- [Section 2 title](#section-2)
- [Section 3 title](#section-3)
- [Appendix or resources](#resources)

---

## Key takeaways

- [Takeaway 1]
- [Takeaway 2]
- [Takeaway 3]

---

## Section 1

[Section intro: what this section covers and why it matters]

### Subsection 1.1

[Content]

### Subsection 1.2

[Content]

**Section summary:** [1-2 sentence recap]

---

## Section 2

[Pattern continues...]

---

## Resources

- [Related content]
- [External references]
- [Tools mentioned]
```

## Alternative: Reference document structure

For content that will be consulted repeatedly:

```md
# [Title] Reference

> **Quick link:** [Most-accessed section](#section)

## Contents
[Alphabetized or by frequency of access]

---

## [Topic A]

**When to use:** [Context]

[Content in concise, scannable format]

**Related:** [Topic B], [Topic C]

---

## [Topic B]

[Pattern continues...]
```

## Alternative: Narrative structure

For content meant to be read linearly (case studies, reports):

```md
# [Title]

## The challenge

[Problem statement]

## The approach

[What was done]

## The results

[Outcomes with data]

## What we learned

[Insights and implications]
```

## Heading hierarchy rules

```md
# H1: Document title (one per document)

## H2: Major sections (main navigation level)

### H3: Subsections within major sections

#### H4: Use sparingly — consider if content should be restructured

##### H5+: Avoid — indicates content needs reorganization
```

## Navigation aid patterns

### Table of contents
```md
## Contents
- [Introduction](#introduction)
- [Part 1: Concept](#part-1-concept)
  - [1.1 Background](#11-background)
  - [1.2 Key terms](#12-key-terms)
- [Part 2: Application](#part-2-application)
```

### Section summaries
```md
---

**Section summary:** [Recap in 1-2 sentences]

**Continue to:** [Next section](#next)

---
```

### Cross-references
```md
For more on this topic, see [Related section](#related-section).

This builds on concepts from [Previous section](#previous-section).
```

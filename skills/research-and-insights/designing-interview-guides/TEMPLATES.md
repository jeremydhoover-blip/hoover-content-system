## Interview script

### Opening (5 minutes)

**Introduction**:
"Hi [Name], thanks for taking the time to speak with me today. I'm [Your name], and I'm a researcher at [Company]. We're talking to people like you to better understand [topic area]. There are no right or wrong answers—I'm just interested in your honest experience and opinions."

**Consent and setup**:
- "Is it okay if I record this session? It helps me focus on our conversation instead of taking notes."
- "The recording will only be used by our research team and won't be shared publicly."
- "Do you have any questions before we begin?"

**Warm-up**:
- "To start, can you tell me a bit about your role and what a typical day looks like for you?"
- Probe: "How long have you been doing this?"

## Concept testing interview guide

```md
# Concept Testing Guide: [Concept Name]

## Study overview
- **Research objective**: Evaluate [concept] for [criteria]
- **Stimuli**: [What participants will see/interact with]
- **Duration**: [Time]
- **Format**: [Format]

## Learning objectives
1. Does [concept] solve the problem users have?
2. What's confusing or unclear about [concept]?
3. What would prevent users from adopting [concept]?

---

## Interview script

### Opening (5 minutes)
[Standard opening]

### Context gathering (10 minutes)
**Before showing concept**:

"Before I show you anything, I'd like to understand your current experience."

- "How do you currently [task the concept addresses]?"
- Probe: "What's frustrating about that process?"
- Probe: "What works well?"

### Concept exposure (15-20 minutes)

**Introduction to concept**:
"I'm going to show you something we're exploring. This isn't a finished product—it's an early concept. I want your honest reaction, including any confusion or criticism."

[Show stimulus]

**First impression** (unprompted):
- "What's your initial reaction?"
- [Wait for response—don't lead]

**Comprehension**:
- "In your own words, what is this?"
- "What do you think you could do with this?"
- Probe: "What makes you say that?"

**Value assessment**:
- "How useful would this be for you?"
- Probe: "What would you use it for?"
- Probe: "What would you NOT use it for?"

**Concerns and barriers**:
- "What questions do you have about this?"
- "What would make you hesitant to use this?"
- Probe: "What would need to change for you to try it?"

### Comparison (if applicable) (5 minutes)
[Show alternative or current state]
- "How does this compare to [current solution/alternative]?"
- "Which would you prefer, and why?"

### Closing (5 minutes)
[Standard closing]

---

## Stimulus presentation notes
- Show concept at [X point] in interview
- Allow [Y seconds] for initial review before questions
- [Any interaction instructions]
```

## Stakeholder interview guide

```md
# Stakeholder Interview Guide: [Initiative/Project]

## Interview details
- **Stakeholder**: [Name, Role]
- **Objective**: Understand [their perspective on X]
- **Duration**: 30-45 minutes

## Questions

### Role and context
1. Can you describe your role and how it connects to [project/product]?
2. What does success look like for you in your role?

### Current state
3. How would you describe the current state of [topic]?
4. What's working well? What's not?

### Needs and priorities
5. What are your top priorities for [timeframe]?
6. What problems would you most like solved?

### Constraints and concerns
7. What constraints do you operate under? (Budget, timeline, dependencies)
8. What concerns do you have about [proposed direction]?

### Definition of success
9. If we succeed with [initiative], what would that look like?
10. How would you measure success?

### Closing
11. Who else should I talk to about this?
12. Is there anything I should have asked but didn't?
```

## Variation guidance
| Element | Required | Flexible |
|---------|----------|----------|
| Learning objectives | Yes, 3-5 | Wording can vary |
| Opening/consent | Yes | Style can vary |
| Core questions | Yes, 5-12 | Number based on duration |
| Probes | Yes, at least 1 per core question | Specific probes flexible |
| Timing estimates | Recommended | Can be rough |
| Closing | Yes | Format can vary |

Introducing Content Context

The golden layer of structured knowledge that captures everything before content is written.

Content Context is a concept I formalized after seeing the same failure patterns across industries: people don't have the information they needed, and AI can't produce consistent output because the context is scattered or lives in people's heads.

A Content Context pack captures feature-level knowledge in a structured format that both humans and AI can use:

Feature states User goals UI inventory Vocabulary Error taxonomy Business rules Success metrics Constraints

The result: AI can write within your product's constraints without you micromanaging every output. And when requirements change, you update the context pack, not dozens of prompts.

content-context.json
// Structured feature knowledge
{
  "feature": "File Upload",
  "purpose": "Allow users to attach documents",
  
  "states": ["empty", "uploading", "success", "error"],
  
  "vocabulary": {
    "preferred": ["upload", "attach"],
    "avoid": ["submit", "send"]
  },
  
  "constraints": {
    "maxFileSize": "25MB",
    "allowedTypes": [".pdf", ".docx"]
  }
}

How it works

1

Load the instructions

Point your AI assistant to the repo. The system includes everything it needs: skills, shared standards, examples, and evaluation criteria.

2

Describe your task

Tell the AI what you need. Error message, landing page, API docs, whatever. It automatically selects and applies the right skills.

3

Use it out of the box, or customize

Works immediately with sensible defaults. Or fork the repo and edit the shared files to match your team's voice, style, and standards.

4

Make it yours

Add your own examples, references, scripts, and evaluation criteria. The system grows with your team and can be embedded into your workflows.
